The Sprinkles Crop Hoodie is queer-owned NYC apparel for the gay men who arrive at every event in a group of three or more — the boys who travel as a unit, dance as a unit, and exit as a unit, ideally with one extra person who wasn't there at the start of the night. "Sprinkles," plural, because it is never just one. We cut this cropped hoodie for the friend group that operates on a single shared brain and a single shared cab pool.

The body is a midweight 8 oz cotton-poly fleece with a brushed interior — soft, warm, holds shape through 3am encounters with curbside puddles. Cropped at the natural waist, with a real drawstring hood and a kangaroo pocket the proper depth. True unisex sizing; runs cropped and slightly relaxed by design. Size down if you want a cleaner silhouette over high-waisted shorts; stay true to size for the intended cropped-oversized drape. The chest print is centered, large, scaled to read across a warehouse dancefloor.

Wear this with the same friends every weekend until the seams give out. Wear it to Bushwick warehouse parties, Fire Island ferry rides, Cherry Grove afterhours, Tompkins Square Park Sunday afternoons, and the morning-after coffee runs where everyone is wearing each other's hoodies and nobody can find their phone. The Sprinkles Crop Hoodie is for the friend groups that doubled as dating pools, the gay sons who became gay siblings, the chosen-family chaos that NYC specializes in.

The whole BLA Sprinkles line lives in this energy.
