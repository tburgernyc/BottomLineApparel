The Bottoms Limited Edition Towel is queer-owned NYC apparel for the gay men whose summer requires actual horizontal real estate. Beach towel by spec, multi-purpose by design — large enough to cover a Fire Island Sunday lounger, soft enough to throw down at the McCarren Park lawn for a daylight nap, absorbent enough to follow you home from the public pool without leaving a wet spot on the F train seat. We made this Limited Edition because some pieces deserve their own dedicated drop, and a beach towel that doubles as group-chat real estate is one of them.

The base is a heavyweight microfiber-poly blend with a brushed cotton-feel face — soft on skin, dries fast, doesn't shed lint after the first wash. Generously sized for a single adult to fully stretch out without becoming a pretzel. The graphic is full-bleed, scaled for visibility from across the boardwalk, printed with high-saturation reactive ink that holds up through chlorine, salt water, and the weekly hot wash. Edged with a clean serged finish that doesn't fray.

Use this gay beach towel at Riis Park, at Cherry Grove, at the public pool in McCarren, at the rooftop pool of the friend who lets you tag along, at the gym after the shower you weren't going to take but did. Use it as a picnic blanket on a Saturday afternoon in Tompkins Square. Use it as a wrap when you've decided you're not putting clothes back on after the swim. The Bottoms Limited Edition Towel works for everyone whose summer involves water and visibility — which, if you're shopping at a queer-owned NYC apparel brand, is probably everyone.

Bottom Line Apparel is queer-owned, NYC-based, and runs Limited Edition drops on print-on-demand from a certified Printful facility — no warehouse, no overstock, no waste. Add the towel to your cart, claim your patch of grass or sand, and stake your flag.
