The Bottom Line Sweatpants are queer-owned NYC apparel for the gay men whose Sunday-morning aesthetic is "I borrowed these and I am not giving them back." The slogan is the brand thesis — yours, ours, his — and we put it on the most flattering, most flexible, most-borrowed garment in the catalog. Soft, structured, cropped at the ankle in a way that flatters every leg, with a drawstring waist that survives both kettlebell sets and the kind of evenings that don't always end where they started.

Built on a midweight cotton-poly fleece with a brushed interior that softens with each wash. Tapered through the leg with a slight cuff at the ankle so the silhouette stays clean over slides, sneakers, or socks-only at home. The waistband is wide and ribbed, holds its shape, and has a real internal drawstring (not just a decorative outer cord). True unisex sizing — the cut is forgiving but tailored; size down if you want a closer drape, stay true to size for the relaxed gym-to-bar fit. The "Bottom Line" graphic sits low on the side leg, scaled to be visible without screaming.

Wear these sweatpants to the gym you joined last month and have been to four times. Wear them on the F train at noon when you've decided not to leave Brooklyn today. Wear them on a Saturday-morning Brooklyn coffee run when the only requirement is "no zippers." Wear them on the rooftop in October over a tank. Wear them home from a sleepover and pretend you're surprised to still be wearing them three days later. The Bottom Line Sweatpants are the most-stolen item in our catalog for a reason.

Bottom Line Apparel is queer-owned, NYC-based, and prints every order on demand at a certified Printful facility. No deadstock, no warehouse, no fast-fashion guilt. Add the Bottom Line Sweatpants to your cart, decide later who they're for, and brace yourself: someone is absolutely going to ask to borrow them.
